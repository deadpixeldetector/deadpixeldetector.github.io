A common problem that can lower the quality of any screen, whether it's a laptop display or a 4K gaming monitor, is a defective pixel. It's critical to differentiate between stuck pixels and dead pixels. A dead pixel is a permanent flaw that shows up as a black area on the screen because the transistor is broken and cannot be fixed. Replacing the screen is the only option in this situation. A stuck pixel, on the other hand, stays fixed on a primary color (red, green, or blue), but software can usually fix it.
